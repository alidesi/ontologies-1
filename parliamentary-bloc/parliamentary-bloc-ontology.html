Affiliationc back to ToC or Class ToC

IRI: http://parliament.uk/ontologies/parliamentary-bloc/Affiliation

A person or group's affiliation to a parliamentary bloc between between two dates. Could be used to describe affiliations to non-political party blocs. e.g. Crossbenchers and bishops in the House of Lords. Could also be used to describe members having an affiliation to the Co-operative Party whilst being whipped to Labour.
has sub-classes
Whippingc
is in domain of

Whippingc back to ToC or Class ToC

IRI: http://parliament.uk/ontologies/parliamentary-bloc/Whipping

A member of a legislature being whipped to a political party between two dates. A member accepting a whip agrees to vote mainly in line with the party they're whipped to. The whip may be withdrawn by the party or rejected by the member.
