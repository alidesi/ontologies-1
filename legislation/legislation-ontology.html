ActOfParliamentc back to ToC or Class ToC

IRI: http://parliament.uk/ontologies/legislation/ActOfParliament

An Act of Parliament creates a new law or changes an existing law. Once a Bill has been approved by both the House of Commons and the House of Lords and been given Royal Assent by the Monarch it becomes an Act of Parliament. Taken together, Acts of Parliament make up what is known as Statute Law in the UK.
is in domain of
delegatesop, specifiesDayCountop

DayCountc back to ToC or Class ToC

IRI: http://parliament.uk/ontologies/legislation/DayCount

Some Acts of Parliament define a day count during which Parliament may scrutinise an instrument. The days may either be sitting days or statutory days (see the time period ontology) e.g. the Statutory Instruments Act 1946 defines a default period of 40 statutory days of scrutiny for instruments subject to parliamentary procedure. Sometimes the enabling act (via enabling power) may define an exception to this default. Most commonly this will be 28, 60 or 120 days.
is in domain of
countdp, dayTypeop
is in range of
dayCountDefinedByop, specifiesDayCountop

DraftStatutoryInstrumentc back to ToC or Class ToC

IRI: http://parliament.uk/ontologies/legislation/DraftStatutoryInstrument

A draft statutory instrument. Subject to parliamentary approval, this can become an item of secondary legislation if and when a government minister 'makes' it. Despite not (yet) being legislation, draft statutory instruments are published on legislation.gov.uk. Equivalent to a FRBR work in a legislation.gov.uk sense.
is in range of

EnablingPowerc back to ToC or Class ToC

IRI: http://parliament.uk/ontologies/legislation/EnablingPower

A power delegated by one or more items of primary legislation, enabling a government minister to bring forward secondary legislation. The terms under which the secondary legislation can be brought forward, such as the procedure it will be subject to, any override of statutory day count, and the scope of the secondary legislation are specified by the enabling power.
is in range of
delegatesop, enabledByop

MadeStatutoryInstrumentc back to ToC or Class ToC

IRI: http://parliament.uk/ontologies/legislation/MadeStatutoryInstrument

A statutory instrument which has been made into law by a government minister. Statutory instruments are a type of secondary (or delegated) legislation. They can be used to make specific changes to the law under enabling powers delegated by Acts of Parliament.

ProposedNegativeStatutoryInstrumentc back to ToC or Class ToC

IRI: http://parliament.uk/ontologies/legislation/ProposedNegativeStatutoryInstrument

All regulations proposed to be made under the powers in clauses 7, 8 or 9 of the European Union (Withdrawal) Bill, which Ministers consider need to be made under the negative procedure, will be laid before both Houses as proposed statutory instruments. Sifting committees in both Houses may recommend a change to the affirmative procedure. A proposed statutory instrument may lead to a statutory instrument being laid, or multiple, or none. This is subject to change given amendments made to the EU Withdrawal Bill.

WorkPackagec back to ToC or Class ToC

IRI: http://parliament.uk/ontologies/legislation/WorkPackage

A package of work grouping together many individual business items e.g. the passage of a specific statutory instrument, or the passage of a specific public bill etc. Whereas a procedure (and its accompanying routes and steps) describes the process, the work package handles the application of that process for a specific instance.

comingIntoForceDatedp back to ToC or Data Property ToC

IRI: http://parliament.uk/ontologies/legislation/comingIntoForceDate

The date on which the statutory instrument came, or is going to come, into force. Where given, this will be the date on which the whole statutory instrument comes into force. Sometimes coming into force will be fuzzier than a given date e.g. different schedules within the SI will come into force at different times. In these cases there'll be no comingIntoForceDate; the coming into force details will be given as a comingIntoForceNote.

IRI: http://parliament.uk/ontologies/legislation/siYear

The year of a statutory instrument. Usually this will be the year it was laid before Parliament.
