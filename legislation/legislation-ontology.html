An item of secondary legislation. Secondary legislation is also referred to as delegated legislation. The terms are synonymous. The term delegated refers to the fact that the powers claimed by the legislation are delegated from another item of legislation.

IRI: http://parliament.uk/ontologies/statutory-instrument/StatutoryInstrument

A statutory instrument. Statutory instruments are a type of delegated (or secondary) legislation. They can be used to make specific changes to the law under under authority contained in Acts of Parliament.

enabledByop back to ToC or Object Property ToC

IRI: http://parliament.uk/ontologies/statutory-instrument/enabledBy

Relates a statutory instrument to the item of legislation or items of legislation which enable it. The enabling legislation delegates legislative powers to the statutory instrument. Note that sometimes a statutory instrument will be enabled by another piece of secondary legislation. But that must in turn be enabled by an Act (a piece of primary legislation). IS THIS TRUE. Could it be SI > SI > SI > Act?

comingIntoForceDatedp back to ToC or Data Property ToC

IRI: http://parliament.uk/ontologies/statutory-instrument/comingIntoForceDate

The date on which the SI was or is going to come into force. Where given this will be the date on which the whole SI comes into force. Sometimes coming into force will be fuzzier than a given date. Sometimes different schedules within the SI will come into force at different times. In these cases there'll be no comingIntoForceDate but the coming into force details will be given as a comingIntoForceNote.
