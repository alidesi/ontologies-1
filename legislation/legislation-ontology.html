DayCountc back to ToC or Class ToC

IRI: http://parliament.uk/ontologies/legislation/DayCount

is defined by
http://parliament.uk/ontologies/legislation/
Some Acts of Parliament define a day count during which Parliament may scrutinise a piece of secondary legislation. The days may be sitting days or statutory days (see the time period ontology) e.g. the Statutory Instruments Act 1946 defines a default period of 40 statutory days of scrutiny for instruments subject to parliamentary procedure. Sometimes the enabling Act may define an exception to this default. Most commonly this will be 28, 60 or 120 days. The European Union (Withdrawal) Act 2018 defines a default period of 10 sitting days of scrutiny for proposed negative statutory instruments.
is in domain of
countdp, dayTypeop
is in range of
imposesDayCountDutyop

Made statutory instruments may progress from draft or be laid without draft before Parliament, as made. StatutoryInstrumentSeriesItemc back to ToC or Class ToC

IRI: http://parliament.uk/ontologies/legislation/StatutoryInstrumentSeriesItem

is defined by
http://parliament.uk/ontologies/legislation/
A statutory instrument series item links a draft or made statutory instrument subject to parliamentary procedure to a statutory instrument series. A statutory instrument can be an item in more than one series excepting these cases: Northern Ireland series instruments do not form part of any other series; legal series instruments do not form part of any other series; National Assembly for Wales series SIs and Scottish series instruments can be included in the commencement order series but no other. Commencement series items may also form part of either the Welsh or Scottish series. Where a instrument is an item in more than one series, the citation order is formed by (1) the commencement series and item identifier followed by (2) any other series and item identifier. Where the instrument is made by the Welsh Ministers, the 'W. / Cy.' number follows the SI number.
is in domain of
inSeriesop, siSeriesItemIdentifierdp
is in range of
asSeriesItemop

Relates an Act of Parliament to an enabling power delegated in whole or in part by that Act. http://parliament.uk/ontologies/legislation/
Relates an enabling power to a day count. For statutory instruments the default statutory day count is 40 statutory days, as set out by the Statutory Instruments Act 1946. An enabling Act may override this value. A day count is only set by a single Act of Parliament. A single Act of Parliament may specify differing day counts for different enabling powers. A statutory instrument is required to have a single day count when enabling powers are delegated from more than one Act of Parliament.
