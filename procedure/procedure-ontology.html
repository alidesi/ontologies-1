Routec back to ToC or Class ToC

Ties a procedural step to the subsequent step or steps according to the parliamentary procedure which applies. Note that routes are time bound (borrowing startOn and endOn from the Core Ontology). This allows routes to change over time and adapt to changes in procedure.

RouteTypec back to ToC or Class ToC

IRI: http://parliament.uk/ontologies/procedure/RouteType

Ties a route to a route type. Route types are: causes, allows and precludes. A route of type causes relates the route to a following step where completion of the preceding step(s) directly causes the route to trigger the next step. A route of type allows relates the route to a following step where completion of the preceding step(s) allows the next step to happen, but does not cause it to happen. A route of type precludes relates a route to a step where completion of the preceding step(s) precludes that step from happening.
is in range of
hasRouteTypeop

WorkPackagec back to ToC or Class ToC

IRI: http://parliament.uk/ontologies/procedure/WorkPackage

A package of work grouping together many individual business items e.g. the passage of a specific statutory instrument or the passage of a specific public bill etc. Whereas a procedure (and its accompanying routes and steps) describes the process, the work package handles the application of that process for a specific instance.
